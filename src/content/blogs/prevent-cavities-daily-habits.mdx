## Understanding Dental Cavities

Dental cavities, also known as caries or tooth decay, are permanently damaged areas in the hard surface of your teeth that develop into tiny openings or holes.

They are caused by bacteria, frequent sugary foods, acidic drinks, and poor oral hygiene. When plaque builds up, it produces acids that weaken tooth enamel and create cavities.

---

## Daily Habits to Prevent Cavities

1. Brush your teeth twice daily with fluoride toothpaste  
2. Floss every day  
3. Limit sugary and acidic foods  
4. Drink water frequently  
5. Use antibacterial mouthwash  

---

## Key Takeaways

- Cavities are caused by plaque and sugar interaction  
- Daily brushing and flossing are essential  
- Reducing sugar intake protects enamel  
- Hydration helps cleanse the mouth  
- Regular dental visits prevent long-term damage
